Navigating career 03 / 06
The one thing: structure the problem before jumping to solutions
Founders waste weeks building the wrong thing because they never defined what problem they were actually solving.
This habit from consulting genuinely saves me from myself.
It also means I spend 10 minutes writing the problem statement before I open my laptop. Worth it.
@drawoheriter
Navigating career 04 / 06
What does not transfer: slides, frameworks, consensus
Nobody is waiting for your 12-slide deck. There is no steering committee. There is no billable hour.
Consensus-building is how you die slowly in a startup.
I made a beautiful framework for my kakisewa email strategy. A founder friend said: just send the emails. He was right.
@drawoheriter
Navigating career 05 / 06
The dangerous habit: mistaking analysis for action
Consulting rewards thoroughness. You get paid to be right, not fast.
Startups reward people who move. Being thorough while the runway burns is just expensive thinking.
I have 8,471 agents scraped and zero paying customers. Analysis did not cause that. But it did not help either.
@drawoheriter
