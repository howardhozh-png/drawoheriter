I shipped kakisewa and told exactly zero people about it for two weeks. Not because I was doing a quiet launch strategy. Because I was terrified. Imposter syndrome when you're a founder hits different from when you're a consultant. In consulting, someone above you signs off. Someone validates the work. The deck gets approved. You're protected by the process. When it's your product, there's no one to approve it. It's just you and whatever you built and the very real possibility that it reflects exactly how you think — and your thinking is wrong. I pushed through it the only way I knew how. Shipped anyway. Waited for the market to tell me I was an idiot. Still waiting, honestly. But at least the thing exists now. Have you ever felt like a fraud doing something you were actually capable of?
