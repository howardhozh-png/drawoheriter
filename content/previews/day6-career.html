Navigating career 03 / 06
Reason 1: You are wired to reduce risk, not take it.
Consulting rewards you for spotting what can go wrong and building a slide about it.
Founding rewards you for shipping anyway. These are opposite instincts.
I have a 3-tab spreadsheet analysing cold email risk. I should have just sent the emails two weeks earlier.
@drawoheriter
Navigating career 04 / 06
Reason 2: You are used to clients defining the problem.
In consulting, someone hands you the question. Your job is to answer it well.
As a founder, choosing the right problem to solve is the whole job. Nobody hands you that.
I kept waiting for my users to tell me exactly what to build. They do not know either. That was a surprise.
@drawoheriter
Navigating career 05 / 06
Reason 3: Optimised for looking right, not being right.
MBB trains you to never walk into a room without the answer already locked.
Founding means being publicly wrong, repeatedly, and updating fast. The deck is never done.
kakisewa.com is live. Zero paying customers. This post exists. Make of that what you will.
@drawoheriter
